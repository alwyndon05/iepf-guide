How to Claim Your Shares from IEPF: A Complete Step-by-Step Guide

Everything you need to know about recovering your unclaimed shares and dividends - written in simple language for everyone

3-6 months process
Zero government fees
100% recoverable

Why This Guide?

Over Rs. 76,000 crore worth of shares and dividends lie unclaimed in India. Many people don't even know they have money waiting for them. If you or a family member invested in shares years ago and lost track, there's a good chance some of that money is sitting with the government.

The good news? You can get it all back. This guide will walk you through every step of the process in simple, easy-to-understand language.

1 What is IEPF?

IEPF stands for Investor Education and Protection Fund. It's a fund managed by the Government of India under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs.

How shares end up in IEPF:

When dividends or shares remain unclaimed for 7 consecutive years, companies are legally required to transfer them to IEPF. This happens because:

  • Your address changed and dividend cheques couldn't reach you
  • Old physical share certificates were misplaced
  • Bank account details were not updated
  • The original investor passed away and family wasn't aware
  • Demat account became inactive or was forgotten
Good News!

Transfer to IEPF doesn't mean you've lost your money. You can claim it back anytime - there's no deadline!

2 How to Check if You Have Unclaimed Shares

Before starting the claim process, you need to verify if your shares are actually with IEPF. Here are the ways to check:

Method 1

Search on IEPF Website

  1. Go to www.iepf.gov.in
  2. Click on "Know Your IEPF" or "Search Unclaimed Dividends"
  3. Enter your name/folio number/DPID-Client ID
  4. If records appear, your shares are with IEPF
Method 2

Check Company's Website

  1. Visit the investor relations section of the company's website
  2. Look for "Unclaimed Dividends" or "IEPF" section
  3. Companies publish lists of shareholders whose shares are transferred to IEPF
Method 3

Contact the Registrar & Transfer Agent (RTA)

Every company has an RTA that manages its shareholder records. Common RTAs include:

  • KFin Technologies (formerly Karvy)
  • Link Intime India
  • Bigshare Services
  • NSDL/CDSL (for demat shares)

Contact them with your folio number or DP ID to check your status.

Pro Tip: Search using different name variations (with/without middle name) as records may be stored differently.

3 Before You Start: Gather These Documents

Having all documents ready before you begin will make the process much smoother. Here's what you'll need:

Identity Documents

  • Aadhaar Card Self-attested photocopy
  • PAN Card Self-attested photocopy (mandatory)
  • Passport-size Photo Recent photograph

Bank Documents

  • Cancelled Cheque With your name printed on it
  • Bank Passbook Copy First page with account details

Share-Related Documents

  • Share Certificate Original if you have physical shares
  • Client Master List (CML) From your DP (for demat shares)
  • Folio Number / DP ID Your unique identifier

Legal Documents

  • Indemnity Bond On non-judicial stamp paper (Rs. 100-500)
  • Advance Stamped Receipt On stamp paper
Important: Client Master List (CML)

If you have demat shares, you MUST get a CML from your Depository Participant (like Zerodha, Groww, HDFC Securities, etc.). This is a one-page document containing your demat account details. You can usually download it from your broker's app or website.

4 Step 1: Register on MCA Portal

Create Your Account on MCA Website

The Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) website is where you'll file your claim. First, you need to register.

1

Go to MCA Portal

Visit www.mca.gov.in

Click on "MCA Services" in the top menu

2

Click on "Register"

Select "Registered User" or "New User" option

Choose "Indian National" as your category

3

Fill Registration Details

Enter your personal details:

  • Full Name (as per PAN)
  • Date of Birth
  • PAN Number
  • Email Address
  • Mobile Number
  • Create Password
4

Verify Your Account

You'll receive OTPs on your email and mobile

Enter both OTPs to complete registration

Save your User ID and password safely

MCA Registration Page

Visit mca.gov.in and look for the "Register" or "Sign Up" button in the top navigation area

5 Step 2: Fill Form IEPF-5 Online

Complete the Online Application Form

Form IEPF-5 is the official application to claim your shares. Here's how to fill it correctly:

1

Login to MCA Portal

Use your registered email/user ID and password

2

Navigate to IEPF Section

Go to: MCA Services > IEPF > Claim Refund

Or search for "IEPF-5" in the forms section

3

Fill Company Details

Company Name Full name of the company whose shares you're claiming
CIN Number Corporate Identity Number (find on company's website or MCA portal)
4

Fill Claimant Details

Claimant Name Your name exactly as per PAN card
Relationship Self / Legal Heir / Nominee / Joint Holder
Address Current residential address
PAN Your PAN number
Aadhaar Your 12-digit Aadhaar number
5

Fill Share/Dividend Details

Folio Number For physical shares - found on share certificate
DP ID & Client ID For demat shares - from your CML
Number of Shares Total shares being claimed
Certificate Numbers For physical shares - from share certificate
6

Fill Bank Details

Bank Name Your bank's full name
Account Number Account where dividends will be credited
IFSC Code 11-character code of your bank branch
7

Upload Documents

Upload scanned copies of:

  • Aadhaar Card
  • PAN Card
  • Cancelled Cheque
  • Share Certificate (if applicable)
  • CML (if applicable)

File size: Usually max 2-5 MB per file, PDF format preferred

8

Submit and Get SRN

Review all details carefully

Click "Submit" to file the form

You'll receive an SRN (Service Request Number)

Save this SRN - you'll need it to track your claim!

Important Note:

One Form IEPF-5 is for one company only. If you have shares in multiple companies, you need to file separate forms for each.

6 Step 3: Prepare Physical Documents

Prepare Your Physical Submission Package

After online submission, you need to send physical copies of documents to the company. Here's how to prepare them:

Physical Document Checklist

1. Form IEPF-5 (Printed & Signed)
  • Download the acknowledgment/form from MCA portal
  • Print on A4 paper
  • Sign at all designated places
  • Do NOT leave any signature boxes empty
2. Indemnity Bond
  • Must be on non-judicial stamp paper
  • Stamp paper value: Rs. 100-500 (varies by state)
  • Use the format provided by the company/RTA
  • Sign in presence of a notary public
  • Get it notarized
Where to get stamp paper: Any licensed stamp vendor, court premises, or some banks sell stamp papers.
3. Advance Stamped Receipt
  • On stamp paper of appropriate value
  • Acknowledges you'll receive the shares/dividends
  • Format usually provided by company
4. Self-Attested Document Copies
  • Aadhaar card - both sides
  • PAN card
  • Cancelled cheque OR bank passbook first page
  • Client Master List (for demat shares)
  • Share certificate copy (for physical shares)

"Self-attested" means sign on the copy and write "Self-attested" or "Attested" with date

5. Original Share Certificate
  • Submit original if you have physical shares
  • If lost, submit an affidavit stating the same
  • May need to publish a newspaper notice for lost certificates
Pro Tip: Keep photocopies of everything you send. Take photos of the entire package before sealing.

7 Step 4: Submit to Company/RTA

Send Your Documents

Where to Send Documents?

You have two options:

Option A: Company's Registered Office

Send to the company's registered office address. Find this on:

  • Company's website (Investor Relations section)
  • MCA website - company search
  • Annual reports
Option B: Registrar & Transfer Agent (RTA)

Most companies prefer you send to their RTA. Contact company to confirm RTA details.

How to Send

  • Use Speed Post or Registered Post - You get a tracking number
  • Keep the receipt - Proof of dispatch
  • Write clearly on envelope:

To,

The Company Secretary / Nodal Officer (IEPF)

[Company Name]

[Full Address]


Subject: IEPF Claim - SRN: [Your SRN Number]

What Happens Next?

Company receives documents 1-2 weeks after dispatch
Verification by Company 2-8 weeks
Company submits report to IEPF After verification
IEPF Authority Review 4-12 weeks
Shares/Dividends Credited After IEPF approval

8 Step 5: Track Your Claim

Monitor Your Claim Status

Track on MCA Portal

  1. Login to www.mca.gov.in
  2. Go to "Track Transaction Status"
  3. Enter your SRN number
  4. View current status

Track on IEPF Portal

  1. Visit www.iepf.gov.in
  2. Look for "Track Claim" section
  3. Enter your details

Contact Company/RTA

If no update for 3-4 weeks, contact the company's investor relations or RTA to check status.

Understanding Claim Status

Pending Form submitted, awaiting company verification
Under Process Company is verifying documents
Sent to IEPF Company has forwarded to IEPF Authority
Approved Claim approved, assets will be credited soon
Rejected Issue with claim - check reason and resubmit

9 Special Cases: Heirs, Nominees & Joint Holders

If you're not the original shareholder, you'll need additional documents:

Legal Heir

When the original shareholder has passed away:

  • Death Certificate - Original or attested copy
  • Succession Certificate - From civil court, OR
  • Probate - If there's a will, OR
  • Legal Heir Certificate - From tehsildar/municipality
  • NOC from Other Heirs - If multiple heirs exist
  • Affidavit - Declaring you're the legal heir
Note: For small claims (usually under Rs. 2 lakhs), some companies may accept a legal heir certificate instead of succession certificate.

Nominee

If you were registered as nominee:

  • Death Certificate - Of the shareholder
  • Nomination Form - Copy showing your nomination
  • Affidavit - Confirming your nominee status
  • Your Identity Documents
Note: Nominee has precedence over legal heirs for claiming shares.

Joint/Surviving Holder

If you're a co-holder and one holder has passed away:

  • Death Certificate - Of deceased holder(s)
  • Declaration - From surviving holder(s)
  • Identity Documents - Of all surviving holders
Note: Shares pass automatically to surviving joint holders - this is usually simpler than legal heir claims.

10 Common Mistakes to Avoid

Name Mismatch

Name on form should match PAN exactly. Even small differences (Mr./Shri, initials vs full name) can cause rejection.

Fix: Use name exactly as printed on PAN card.

Wrong Demat Details

Entering incorrect DP ID or Client ID means shares can't be credited.

Fix: Double-check from your CML. DP ID is 8 characters, Client ID is 8 characters.

Missing Indemnity Bond

Forgetting the indemnity bond or using wrong format.

Fix: Use the exact format provided by company. Get it notarized.

Incomplete Heir Documents

Not providing proper succession/legal heir certificate.

Fix: Check with company first what they accept. Some accept legal heir certificate for small claims.

Paying Unnecessary Fees

Paying agents or "consultants" to file claims.

Fix: The IEPF claim process is FREE. You only pay for stamp paper and courier. Don't pay anyone claiming to expedite your claim.

Filing Multiple Claims Incorrectly

Filing multiple forms for the same company or mixing up companies.

Fix: One form per company. If you have shares in 5 companies, file 5 separate forms.
