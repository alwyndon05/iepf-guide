IEPF Claim Guide

Everything you need to know about claiming your shares

What is IEPF?

The Investor Education and Protection Fund is a statutory fund under the Companies Act, 2013. It's administered by the IEPF Authority under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs.

  • Safeguard — Protects unclaimed assets from being lost
  • Centralize — Maintains proper custody and records
  • Enable Recovery — Lets rightful owners reclaim anytime

Why do shares get transferred?

Shares and dividends are transferred after remaining unclaimed for 7 consecutive years.

  • Address or contact details changed
  • Physical certificates misplaced
  • Investor passed away, heirs unaware
  • Old demat accounts became inactive
Key Point: Ownership isn't lost. You can reclaim anytime — there's no deadline.

What can be transferred to IEPF?

  • Dividends — Uncollected dividend amounts
  • Shares — Equity and preference shares
  • Deposits — Matured company fixed deposits
  • Debentures — Bonds and interest payments
  • IPO Refunds — Application money due back

Physical vs Demat Shares

Physical
  • Certificate details critical
  • Folio number needed
  • May need dematerialisation
  • Process is slower
Demat
  • DP ID & Client ID needed
  • CML is mandatory
  • Process is smoother
  • Direct credit to account

Signs you might have unclaimed shares:

  • Invested years ago and lost track
  • Have old physical certificates
  • Family member invested and passed away
  • Dividends stopped arriving unexpectedly

How to find out: Check company websites for unclaimed dividend lists, search the IEPF website database, or contact the RTA directly.

  • Company verification: 2-8 weeks
  • IEPF processing: 4-12 weeks
  • Total: 3-6 months

Legal heir cases often take longer due to additional verification. Simple self-claims are usually faster.

  • Name mismatch across documents
  • Incomplete heir documentation
  • Wrong demat details
  • Missing indemnity bond format
  • Multiple claims filed incorrectly
Most rejections are procedural. You can correct and resubmit.
  • One claim per company per form
  • Shares + dividends can be claimed together
  • IEPF charges zero fees
  • Rejected claims can be resubmitted
  • Be cautious of agents charging high fees
